About Me

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Technology at the Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, where I will work with the State Key Laboratory of Processor Chips. I received my B.S. degree from ShanghaiTech University, with coursework and project experience in digital integrated circuits, computer architecture, advanced analog integrated circuits, programming, and communication systems.

My current interests lie at the intersection of computer architecture and integrated circuit design, especially processor chips, die-to-die chiplet interconnects, RISC-V systems, and mixed-signal circuit design.

Research Interests

  • Chiplet Interconnects: die-to-die interfaces, UCIe-like protocols, high-speed transmitter design, and package-aware links.
  • Computer Architecture: RISC-V processors, pipeline design, memory hierarchy, cache behavior, and architecture-level optimization.
  • Integrated Circuits: digital IC design, analog/mixed-signal circuits, low-power design, and PVT-aware circuit optimization.

Selected Projects

24Gb/s Die-to-Die Chiplet Interconnect Transmitter | Loongson Technology

  • Designed a high-speed TX using 1/4-rate clocking and an SST-driver architecture for low-power die-to-die communication.
  • Collaborated on an 8-lane D2D link following UCIe 3.0-style requirements, including PLL, RX, digital control, and cross-die CPU communication.
  • Targeted a standard package with bump pitch Px = 130um and Py = 210um; tape-out is planned in TSMC 12nm technology.

Five-Stage Pipelined RV32I CPU

  • Implemented a classic IF/ID/EX/MEM/WB five-stage pipeline based on a Harvard architecture.
  • Supported R/I/S/B/U/J instruction formats and 40+ RV32I integer instructions.
  • Implemented forwarding, pipeline stall, and branch flush mechanisms; passed official RISC-V riscv-tests with CPI no greater than 1.4.

PGA and Low-Pass Filter Design | UC Berkeley

Advisor: Osama Shana'a, MediaTek

  • Designed a differential-input differential-output programmable gain amplifier and Butterworth low-pass filter in a 45nm educational process.
  • Optimized power, gain range, bandwidth variation, output noise, P1dB, IM3, leakage, and input offset across PVT conditions.
  • Received The Best Circuit Design Award as the first undergraduate recipient of this doctoral-level circuit competition in recent years.
